Together we can do more

We gather active, proactive and constructive people to improve the environment in which we live. We work so that tomorrow we can live better than today!

Who We Are

We are children, teenagers, parents, women, men, grandparents, volunteers and dedicated people who want to live in wilder conditions and work hard to achieve it in the hole country Bulgaria. Club for the Future is created as a natural continuation of Caps for the Future, but concentrated locally.

What We Do

We run a range of high-impact campaigns and events that support premature babies, mountain victims, public awareness of first aid and nature. We started from a single cap, and now we are a whole society that is united under one cause - to participate in the improvement of living conditions in the country.

How To Involve

There are so many ways to make positive change for our country. Volunteer with us. Learn first aid. Don't forget to collect caps, jugs and plastic bottles. Donate blood. Make a donation to our organization.

Where We Work

The idea for Clubs for the Future comes from Lazar Radkov (the founder of "Caps for the Future") and the purpose of the clubs is to act locally in every city in the country where there is at least one person ready to start a club and work to improve the environment.

Our impact

Our main goal is to improve the living environment around us, we do it voluntarily and are guided by the motivation "Let tomorrow be better than today". Our organization play a crucial role in addressing societal challenges, promoting positive change, and making a significant impact on the lives of countless people, animals, and the environment.

Recent days many transported babies

In recent days, the #CapsForFuture ambulance in Plovdiv has been transporting babies in need to Plovdiv or Sofia literally every day! Thank you!

Transported baby from Parvomai

A baby was born on 28.07.2023 in Parvomai, but it turned out to have respiratory problems. He had to be urgently transported to Plovdiv! From there, they immediately sent the #CapsForFuture ambulance! It was necessary to act very quickly!

Donated transport incubator in Kyustendil

With an oxygen module, with an air humidifier, with a heating system, sensors for the temperature of the air and the baby's skin, a battery for when there is no electricity nearby, a comfortable stroller ... Donated to the hospital in Kyustendil by all the people who they help #CapsForTheFuture.

Baby from Velingrad

On 03.07.2023, a SEVENTH (7) MONTH (!!!) woman in labor arrived at the hospital in Velingrad with a detached placenta!! After an emergency C-section, a little one appears in the world, however, in a critical condition. The doctors had to drain nearly 200ml of blood from the baby's stomach, after which the little hero was intubated and with the help of the incubator donated by #CapsForFuture and the local Lion's Club, he was successfully transported to the hospital in Pazardzhik! Mother and baby are fine!

Baby from Karlovo

A baby was born on 26.05.2023 in Karlovo with respiratory failure. The #CapsForFuture ambulance from Plovdiv is leaving immediately! In it are Dr. Koichev, senior nurse Ikimova and paramedic Ivan Lesov, who is one of the people who most often travel with the ambulance. The medics took the baby, breathed him all the way and successfully transported him to the neonatology unit at St. George in Plovdiv. Another successful mission for the ambulance and its team. Another life saved!

Donated monitors in Burgas

On 19.05.2023 we helped to the invasive cardiology department in Burgas to monitor the condition of those treated there by new and advanced vital signs monitors.

Transported twins from Velingrad

On 15.05.2023 the transport incubator that #CapsForFuture donated to the hospital in Velingrad, together with the local Lion's Club, carried out another successful mission by transporting two newborn twins - each weighing less than 2 kg - to the hospital in Pazardzhik ❤ So far, the incubator has helped close to 60 children to safely pass the bends between Velingrad and Pazardzhik.
